WHERE YOU END AND I BEGIN

poem by Lexi Caruso

Sometimes I wonder
When I'm grocery shopping
If I'll walk past you
And not even realize it.

I'm not sure we would
Recognize eachother
For the years have changed us
In more ways than one.

The I hate I held for you
Has been long gone.
For awhile, it projected onto myself-
a little reminder inside my head saying
"You are an imposter."

I've spent more time without you
And yet I still somehow remember
The sound of your voice.

I don't hold onto the bad anymore.
Only the good.
Playing tennis,
Picking out movies at Blockbuster,
Playing drumset while you played guitar,
Eating at your favorite Chinese restaurant.

It takes a long time to understand
The things that happened to me as a kid.
I'm not sure I'll ever have all the answers
And I don't blame anyone anymore.

All I know now is I am thankful
For all the things you taught me.

I am thankful I've reached a point now
Where I can tell where you end
And I begin.
